2. Definition of Key Infrastructure Terms

To maintain operational precision, technical network terms are used frequently throughout these clauses.

  • Firewall Configuration: The specific ruleset that determines what traffic is allowed into or out of your business network.
  • Network Segmentation: Dividing a company network into smaller, isolated zones to keep security threats from spreading between departments.
  • VPN (Virtual Private Network): A secure, encrypted tunnel that lets remote staff connect safely to your office servers.
  • MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication): A security step requiring two or more verification proofs before granting access to network apps.
  • Malware and Ransomware: Malicious software designed by criminals to lock files, steal intellectual property, or disrupt corporate systems.
  • Perimeter Defense: The combination of hardware and software tools guarding the boundary between your private network and public internet.
  • Static IP Allocation: Assigning a fixed, unchangeable digital address to a specific corporate server, printer, or office router.
  • Technical Provisioning: The act of setting up, licensing, and configuring hardware or software systems for operational use.
  • Infrastructure Metadata: Operational routing tables, MAC addresses, and equipment logs that show how data moves through your network.
  • Service Level Agreement (SLA): A formal commitment specifying the network uptime targets, response speeds, and engineering support timelines guaranteed.
  • Data Controller: The organisation that decides how and why any personal or corporate information is processed and stored.
